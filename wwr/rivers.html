Cañete River

Lunahuana offers a range of attractions for all tastes, the best alternative near Lima. Rafting is undoubtedly the star activity in the Lunahuaná Valley, children take the opportunity to bathe and enjoy the tour, while adults enjoy the scenic spectacle that the Cañete River offers us and puts their skills to the test. Do not stay with the desire to experience this great adventure and dare to go rafting in Lunahuana.

Majes River

It has the longest course of the Pacific slope with 450 km in length, it has its origin in the vicinity of the high cruise, at the summit of the western chain of the Andes. In its upper course it is called the Colca River, and forms an inter-Andean valley of appreciable extension. As it descends towards the coast, it takes the name of the Majes River and presents a narrow and deep channel, known by the name of the Colca or Majes canyon, one of the most impressive on earth due to its depth. On the coast it is called Río Camaná and forms a narrow rice valley. It is especially renowned for its shrimp.

Chili River

From its birth in the icy heights, through its long journey through increasingly warm lands, until its arrival at the sea, the Chili River has always been the main protagonist of the existence of the city of Arequipa. Above all, it has been and is the main source of water for its agriculture and the supply of its population; it is also an essential resource for power generation, mining and industry in the province, as well as being the predominant factor in the valley's ecological subsistence.
